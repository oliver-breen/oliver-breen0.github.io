CompTIA Security+ Study Guide

Published on January 28, 2026

The CompTIA Security+ certification is one of the most recognized entry-level cybersecurity certifications. It validates foundational knowledge in security concepts, tools, and best practices. This guide shares my study approach, resources, and tips for passing the exam.

About the Security+ Certification

Why Security+?

  • Industry Recognition: Widely accepted by employers and government agencies
  • DoD Approved: Meets DoD 8570.01-M requirements for IAT Level II
  • Career Foundation: Opens doors to cybersecurity roles
  • Vendor Neutral: Not tied to specific products or platforms
  • Practical Focus: Tests real-world security skills

Exam Details

  • Exam Code: SY0-701 (current version as of 2026)
  • Questions: Maximum of 90 questions
  • Question Types: Multiple choice and performance-based
  • Duration: 90 minutes
  • Passing Score: 750 out of 900 (scaled score)
  • Cost: Approximately $392 USD
  • Validity: 3 years (requires continuing education)

Exam Domains and Objectives

Domain 1: General Security Concepts (12%)

Foundation concepts in cybersecurity:

  • Security Controls: Technical, managerial, operational, and physical
  • CIA Triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
  • Non-Repudiation: Proof of origin and delivery
  • AAA: Authentication, Authorization, Accounting
  • Gap Analysis: Identifying security deficiencies
  • Zero Trust: "Never trust, always verify" model

Domain 2: Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Mitigations (22%)

Understanding and addressing security threats:

  • Threat Actors: Nation-states, hacktivists, insiders, competitors
  • Attack Vectors: Phishing, malware, social engineering
  • Vulnerability Types: Software bugs, misconfigurations, weak credentials
  • Application Attacks: Injection, XSS, CSRF, buffer overflow
  • Network Attacks: DDoS, MitM, DNS poisoning
  • Wireless Attacks: Evil twin, deauth, WPS attacks
  • Cryptographic Attacks: Brute force, rainbow tables, collision

Domain 3: Security Architecture (18%)

Designing secure systems and networks:

  • Security Infrastructure: Firewalls, IDS/IPS, proxies, VPNs
  • Network Segmentation: VLANs, DMZ, air gaps
  • Cloud Security: IaaS, PaaS, SaaS security considerations
  • Secure Protocols: TLS, SSH, IPSec, SFTP
  • Wireless Security: WPA3, EAP, 802.1X
  • Load Balancing: Active-active, active-passive

Domain 4: Security Operations (28%)

Day-to-day security tasks and incident response:

  • Security Monitoring: SIEM, log analysis, alerting
  • Vulnerability Management: Scanning, assessment, remediation
  • Incident Response: Preparation, detection, containment, recovery
  • Digital Forensics: Collection, preservation, analysis
  • Automation: Scripting, orchestration, runbooks
  • Backup and Recovery: Full, incremental, differential

Domain 5: Security Program Management and Oversight (20%)

Governance, risk, and compliance:

  • Governance: Policies, standards, procedures
  • Risk Management: Assessment, treatment, monitoring
  • Compliance: GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, SOX
  • Security Awareness: Training programs, phishing simulations
  • Third-Party Risk: Vendor assessments, SLAs
  • Business Continuity: BCP, DRP, testing

Study Plan

Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

  • Read through study guide, one domain at a time
  • Watch corresponding Professor Messer videos
  • Take notes on key concepts and acronyms
  • Create flashcards for terms and definitions

Phase 2: Deep Dive (Weeks 3-6)

  • Complete second pass through material
  • Focus on weak areas identified in phase 1
  • Practice hands-on skills in home lab
  • Complete practice questions after each domain
  • Review explanations for incorrect answers

Phase 3: Practice and Review (Weeks 7-8)

  • Take full-length practice exams
  • Target score: 85%+ before scheduling real exam
  • Review all flagged questions and weak areas
  • Practice performance-based questions
  • Memorize ports, protocols, and acronyms

Phase 4: Final Prep (Week Before Exam)

  • Light review of notes and flashcards
  • Take one final practice exam
  • Review exam objectives checklist
  • Get adequate sleep and prepare mentally

Key Topics to Master

Ports and Protocols

Memorize these common ports:

  • 21: FTP (File Transfer Protocol)
  • 22: SSH (Secure Shell)
  • 23: Telnet
  • 25: SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol)
  • 53: DNS (Domain Name System)
  • 80: HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol)
  • 110: POP3 (Post Office Protocol v3)
  • 143: IMAP (Internet Message Access Protocol)
  • 443: HTTPS (HTTP Secure)
  • 445: SMB (Server Message Block)
  • 3389: RDP (Remote Desktop Protocol)

Cryptography Concepts

  • Symmetric: AES, 3DES, ChaCha20 (same key for encrypt/decrypt)
  • Asymmetric: RSA, ECC, Diffie-Hellman (public/private key pair)
  • Hashing: SHA-256, SHA-3 (one-way function)
  • Digital Signatures: Non-repudiation and integrity
  • PKI: CA, certificates, CRL, OCSP

Authentication Methods

  • Something You Know: Password, PIN
  • Something You Have: Smart card, token
  • Something You Are: Biometrics
  • MFA: Combining two or more factors
  • SSO: Single Sign-On (SAML, OAuth, OpenID)

Security Controls

  • Technical: Firewalls, encryption, IDS/IPS
  • Administrative: Policies, procedures, training
  • Physical: Locks, guards, cameras
  • Preventive: Stop incidents before they occur
  • Detective: Identify incidents
  • Corrective: Respond to and fix incidents

Incident Response Steps

  1. Preparation: Plans, tools, training
  2. Identification: Detect and verify incident
  3. Containment: Limit damage and spread
  4. Eradication: Remove threat from environment
  5. Recovery: Restore systems to normal operation
  6. Lessons Learned: Document and improve

Practice Tips

Study Techniques

  • Active Learning: Don't just read—practice, discuss, teach others
  • Spaced Repetition: Review material at increasing intervals
  • Flashcards: Use Anki or Quizlet for memorization
  • Mind Maps: Visualize relationships between concepts
  • Study Groups: Discuss topics with peers

Practice Exam Strategy

  • Take practice exams in test-like conditions (timed, no distractions)
  • Review ALL questions, even ones you got right
  • Understand WHY each answer is correct or incorrect
  • Track your weak areas and focus study time there
  • Don't memorize questions—understand concepts

Memorization Techniques

  • Mnemonics: Create memorable phrases for lists
  • Acronyms: Make up acronyms for sequences
  • Association: Link new info to things you know
  • Visualization: Create mental images
  • Repetition: Regular review is key

Exam Strategy

Before the Exam

  • Schedule exam for a time when you're most alert
  • Visit testing center beforehand if possible
  • Prepare two forms of ID
  • Get good sleep the night before
  • Eat a healthy meal before the exam
  • Arrive 15-30 minutes early

During the Exam

  • Brain Dump: Write down key info on scratch paper immediately
  • PBQs First or Last: Some prefer to do performance-based questions last
  • Read Carefully: Watch for words like "BEST", "MOST", "LEAST", "NOT"
  • Eliminate Wrong Answers: Narrow down choices
  • Flag Questions: Mark difficult questions to review later
  • Manage Time: Don't spend too long on any one question
  • Trust Your Gut: First instinct is often correct

Performance-Based Questions (PBQs)

  • These are simulations or drag-and-drop scenarios
  • Read instructions carefully
  • May involve configuring firewalls, analyzing logs, or identifying attacks
  • Practice these beforehand with Dion Training or CertMaster
  • They're worth more points but can be time-consuming

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Focusing Only on Videos: You need multiple study methods
  • Memorizing Without Understanding: Exam tests application, not just recall
  • Ignoring Hands-On Practice: Labs reinforce concepts
  • Not Taking Practice Exams: Essential for exam readiness
  • Studying Too Fast: Give yourself adequate time (2-3 months typical)
  • Neglecting Weak Areas: Focus on what you don't know
  • Exam Anxiety: Practice stress management techniques

After Passing

Continuing Education

Security+ requires renewal every 3 years through:

  • Earning a higher-level certification (e.g., CySA+, CASP+)
  • Accumulating 50 CEUs (Continuing Education Units)
  • Participating in CompTIA CertMaster CE program

Next Steps

Common career paths after Security+:

  • CompTIA CySA+: Cybersecurity Analyst certification
  • CompTIA PenTest+: Penetration testing skills
  • CompTIA CASP+: Advanced security practitioner
  • Vendor-Specific: Cisco, Microsoft, AWS security certs
  • Advanced Certs: CISSP, CEH, OSCP

My Experience and Tips

What Worked for Me

  • Professor Messer's free videos as my primary resource
  • Hands-on practice in my home lab
  • Daily flashcard review (15 minutes)
  • Taking multiple practice exams
  • Focusing extra time on cryptography and risk management
  • Joining online study groups for discussion

Final Advice

  • Consistency: Study a little each day rather than cramming
  • Patience: Don't rush—take time to truly understand concepts
  • Confidence: Trust your preparation on exam day
  • Perspective: If you don't pass first time, learn and try again
  • Application: Think about how concepts apply to real scenarios

Conclusion

The CompTIA Security+ certification is challenging but absolutely achievable with proper preparation. It's not just about passing an exam—it's about building a strong foundation in cybersecurity principles that will serve you throughout your career.

The knowledge you gain studying for Security+ is immediately applicable to real-world security work. Whether you're aiming for SOC analyst, security administrator, or another cybersecurity role, this certification demonstrates your commitment and foundational competence.

Remember: the goal isn't just to pass the exam, but to become a knowledgeable security professional. Use this study time to really understand the concepts, practice the skills, and build confidence in your abilities.

Good luck with your Security+ journey! Feel free to reach out if you have questions or want to discuss study strategies.
